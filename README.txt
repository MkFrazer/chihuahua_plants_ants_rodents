Welcome to the README file for the project, "Evaulating the impact of predator exclusion on Chihuahuan plant species diversity, 1983-2002."

Creator: Madeline Frazer
Association: University of British Columbia - Okanagan, Department of Biology, British Columbia, Canada

The data for this project are from:
S. K. Morgan Ernest, Thomas J. Valone, and James H. Brown. 2009. Long-term monitoring and experimental manipulation of a Chihuahuan Desert ecosystem near Portal, Arizona, USA. Ecology 90:1708.

These data are comprised of plant species abundance data, precipitation data, and treatment data
(presence/absence of large rodents, all rodents, large ants, or all ants).
These data were collected on 24 plots in the Chihuahuan desert near Portal, Arizona, USA
One precipitation data point was obtained for all plots at once. 

Background
Desert ecosystems are also useful models for understanding ecosystem dynamics, such as predator-prey interactions. 
Plants have a variety of predators, including ants and rodents. 
In order to better understand the independent effects, as well as potential additive or synergistic interactions, 
of these predator groups, we used the data collected by Ernest et al. (2009) to analyze how the exclusion of ant 
colonies, rodents, or both influenced plant species diversity on plots in the Chihuahua desert from 1983-2002. 

The 24 plots had a variety of treatments over the years. This project used model comparison to determine if and how
each factor (presence/absence of large rodents, all rodents, PORU ants, and/or all ants; precipitation), affected 
total plant species abundance from 1983-2002.
